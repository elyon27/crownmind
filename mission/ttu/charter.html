TENIO THEOLOGICAL UNIVERSITY

Founding Charter

Veritas et Sapientia — Truth and Wisdom

This document defines TTU’s mandate, governance structure, and Phase I operational scope.

I. Establishment

Tenio Theological University (TTU) is hereby established as a faith-rooted theological and philosophical institution under the covering vision of CrownMind Institute. TTU exists as an academic and formation body dedicated to the pursuit of Truth (Veritas), the cultivation of Wisdom (Sapientia), the formation of disciplined and faithful individuals, and the preservation and transmission of theological, philosophical, and moral knowledge.

II. Mandate of TTU

TTU is founded upon the conviction that education must form both mind and character, and that theological study must produce disciplined living.

Core Mandate

  • Provide structured theological education grounded in Scripture and disciplined inquiry.
  • Promote moral clarity and intellectual integrity.
  • Encourage contemplative reflection, structured obedience, and responsible leadership.
  • Develop modular educational systems accessible in digital form.
  • Preserve doctrinal seriousness while remaining academically structured.

Institutional Motto

Veritas et Sapientia — Truth and Wisdom.

Truth without wisdom leads to pride.
Wisdom without truth leads to compromise.
TTU exists to hold both in disciplined balance.

Foundational Pillars

  • Scriptural Grounding
  • Structured Study
  • Disciplined Practice
  • Moral Formation
  • Legacy Stewardship

III. Governance Structure

1. Founder & Chancellor

The Founder serves as vision bearer and doctrinal guardian, Chancellor of the University, custodian of institutional mandate, and final authority in matters of mission alignment.

  • Protect doctrinal integrity
  • Approve curricular structure
  • Oversee institutional expansion
  • Ensure alignment with the founding mandate

2. Academic Council (Phase I — Foundational Form)

During Phase I, the Academic Council may consist of:

  • Founder/Chancellor
  • Designated Academic Advisor (future role)
  • Module Development Lead

Functions: review and approve course modules, safeguard academic seriousness, ensure coherence across programs, and recommend structural expansion for Phase II.

3. Administrative Oversight

Phase I administration may include:

  • Digital Operations Lead
  • Records & Archive Custodian
  • Communications Steward

Responsibilities: maintain platform integrity, archive institutional documents, and ensure communications align with TTU’s mandate.

4. Future Governance Expansion (Phase II & III)

  • Board of Trustees
  • Faculty Senate
  • Accreditation & Compliance Committee
  • External Academic Partnerships

IV. Phase I Operational Scope

Phase I defines TTU as a Digital Theological Formation Institution. During Phase I, TTU operates as a modular theological learning platform, a structured repository of academic theological materials, and a formation-centered institution without state accreditation (unless formally pursued later).

Phase I Academic Structure

  • Introduction to Scriptural Foundations
  • Theology of Discipline and Formation
  • Moral Philosophy & Conscience
  • History of Christian Thought
  • Structured Obedience & Spiritual Formation

Learning Format

  • PDF Modules
  • Structured Reading Guides
  • Reflection Worksheets
  • Digital Lecture Notes
  • Optional Video Overviews

Certification (Phase I)

TTU may issue Certificates of Completion, Structured Module Recognition, and Internal Formation Acknowledgments. These are non-degree, non-accredited recognitions unless formally registered and accredited in future phases.

Digital Infrastructure

  • Official TTU website
  • Modular HTML academic pages
  • Secure document hosting
  • Structured student communication channels

Financial Structure (Phase I)

  • Free modules and paid advanced modules
  • Donations and supporter contributions
  • Operation under CrownMind Institute infrastructure

All funds shall be stewarded toward platform maintenance, content development, and institutional expansion.

V. Institutional Alignment Clause

TTU shall never become personality-centered, promote self-exaltation over mission, or deviate from its founding mandate. The institution exists not for impression, but for impact.

Faithfulness over visibility.
Structure over hype.
Substance over trend.

VI. Charter Integrity & Amendments

This Charter serves as the foundational governing document of TTU Phase I. It may be amended by formal written revision and requires Founder/Chancellor approval for modification. Any amendment must preserve the original mandate and pillars.

VII. Declaration

Tenio Theological University is established as a disciplined house of study, a structured path of formation, and a steward of truth and wisdom.

Founded under the banner:
Veritas et Sapientia
Bienuel Castillo Tenio
Founder / Chancellor
