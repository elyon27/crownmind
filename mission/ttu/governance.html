Governance & Accountability

Phase I governance is lean but explicit: leadership, counsel, and administration. This creates legitimacy and protects the long-term institutional vision.

Leadership

Office Role Scope (Phase I)
Chancellor / Founder Vision & Mandate Final oversight, doctrinal guardianship, institutional direction.
Academic Council Advisory & Review Curriculum feedback, integrity review, teaching standards.
Registrar Records & Certificates Enrollment logs, completion verification, certificate issuance.
Operations Systems & Logistics Digital campus upkeep, resources, publishing, processes.
Phase I Build Rule: Keep governance simple, documented, and reviewable. Complexity is introduced only when the system can carry it.

Council Structure (Prototype)

  • 2–5 external theological advisors (remote acceptable)
  • 1 external reviewer for major program changes (optional)
  • Annual curriculum review and compliance check
  • Published standards for certificate issuance

Future-Ready Notes

This governance layout is built to scale into Phase II: Dean offices, faculty roles, research standards, degree catalog approvals, and accreditation documentation workflows.
