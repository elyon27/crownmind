The Supremacy of Christ and the Humility of the Servant

A Theological Commentary on Matthew 5:9

TTU Exegetical Commentary

“Blessed are the peacemakers, for they shall be called sons of God.”
— Gospel of Matthew 5:9

Key Doctrine

Peacemaking as the Manifestation of God’s Character in the Life of Believers

This Beatitude, spoken by Jesus Christ in the Sermon on the Mount, reveals that those who actively pursue peace reflect the character of God and therefore bear the identity of His children. The doctrine emphasized in this verse is that true peacemaking arises from participation in the reconciling work of God and serves as evidence of belonging to His family. Peacemakers do not merely avoid conflict; they actively pursue reconciliation, restoration, and harmony in relationships that have been fractured by sin.

Exposition
1. The Development of Kingdom Character

Within the sequence of the Beatitudes in the Gospel of Matthew, the quality of peacemaking emerges as a natural result of the inner transformation described in the preceding statements. The Beatitudes describe a progressive formation of character:

- the poor in spirit acknowledge their dependence upon God
- those who mourn grieve over sin
- the meek submit themselves humbly to God
- those who hunger for righteousness seek God’s will
- the merciful demonstrate compassion
- the pure in heart pursue integrity

Out of this transformed inner life emerges the ability to pursue peace in relationships with others.

Thus peacemaking represents the outward expression of the inner transformation produced by the grace of God.

2. The Meaning of Peace in Biblical Theology

“the peacemakers”

In biblical thought, peace extends far beyond the absence of conflict. Peace refers to the restoration of harmony between individuals, communities, and ultimately between humanity and God. Peacemakers therefore participate in the work of restoring broken relationships. This includes:

- reconciliation between individuals
- healing divisions within communities
- proclaiming the message of reconciliation between humanity and God

Thus peacemaking reflects the broader mission of God to restore creation to its intended harmony.

3. The Active Nature of Peacemaking

The term “peacemakers” emphasizes active engagement rather than passive avoidance of conflict. A peacemaker does not merely remain neutral or disengaged from disputes. Instead, the peacemaker intentionally seeks to restore relationships that have been damaged by sin, pride, or injustice. This process often requires courage, humility, and patience. True peacemaking involves confronting conflict in a spirit of humility and guiding opposing parties toward reconciliation.

4. The Divine Model of Peacemaking

The ultimate example of peacemaking is found in the redemptive work of Jesus Christ. Through His life, death, and resurrection, Christ reconciles humanity to God. The gospel itself is therefore a message of peace—the announcement that reconciliation with God is available through Christ. Believers who participate in this ministry of reconciliation reflect the character of the God who initiates peace with humanity.

5. The Promise of Divine Sonship

“for they shall be called sons of God”

The promise attached to this Beatitude identifies peacemakers as those who bear the likeness of God. In biblical thought, the phrase “sons of God” signifies those who reflect the character and mission of their Father. Since God is the ultimate source of reconciliation and peace, those who pursue peace demonstrate their relationship with Him. Thus peacemaking becomes evidence of belonging to the family of God.

Greek Notes

Several Greek terms illuminate the meaning of this Beatitude.

“μακάριοι” (makarioi)
Translated “blessed,” this term refers to the state of divine favor experienced by those who live according to the values of God’s kingdom.

“εἰρηνοποιοί” (eirēnopoioi)
The word translated “peacemakers” combines the concept of peace (eirēnē) with the act of making or producing it. It refers to those who actively work to establish peace.

“υἱοὶ Θεοῦ” (huioi Theou)
Translated “sons of God,” this phrase indicates those who share in the character and mission of God.

Together these expressions reveal that those who actively pursue reconciliation reflect the nature of God and receive recognition as His children.

Ministry Implications

For students and ministers trained within Tenio Theological University and the mission of Narrow Gate Christian Ministry, this passage offers several essential insights.

1. Ministry Must Pursue Reconciliation
The church must actively work toward restoring relationships that have been damaged by conflict or division.

2. Peacemaking Requires Courage and Wisdom
Those who seek peace often enter complex and difficult situations that require humility, patience, and discernment.

3. The Gospel Is Fundamentally a Message of Reconciliation
Evangelism involves proclaiming the peace that God has established through Christ.

4. Believers Reflect God’s Character Through Peacemaking
When Christians pursue reconciliation, they demonstrate the character of the God who reconciled humanity to Himself.

Concluding Reflection

In the Sermon on the Mount recorded in the Gospel of Matthew, Jesus Christ declares that peacemakers are truly blessed. In a world often shaped by conflict, division, and hostility, those who pursue reconciliation stand as witnesses to the transforming power of God’s kingdom. By restoring broken relationships and proclaiming the peace made possible through Christ, believers reflect the character of their heavenly Father. Thus those who devote themselves to the work of reconciliation reveal themselves to be true children of God, participating in the divine mission of restoring peace to a fractured world.
