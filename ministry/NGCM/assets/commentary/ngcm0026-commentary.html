The True Family of God: Obedience and Spiritual Kinship

A Theological Commentary on Mark 3:35

TTU Exegetical Commentary

“For whoever does the will of God is My brother and My sister and mother.”
— Gospel of Mark 3:35

Key Doctrine

Spiritual Kinship Defined by Obedience to the Will of God

In this statement, Jesus Christ redefines the nature of true family by placing obedience to God above biological relationships. While earthly family ties remain significant, Christ reveals that the deeper and more enduring bond among believers is formed through shared commitment to the will of God. The doctrine emphasized in this passage is that those who align their lives with the will of God become members of the spiritual family established through the mission of Christ. Thus the community of faith transcends social, ethnic, and biological boundaries, forming a new covenant family centered upon obedience to God.

Exposition
1. The Narrative Context

This declaration occurs within a moment of tension recorded in Gospel of Mark 3. As Christ teaches and ministers to a gathered crowd, His biological family arrives seeking to speak with Him. When informed that His mother and brothers are outside waiting for Him, Christ responds by directing attention to those gathered around Him and stating that those who do the will of God are His true family. This response does not diminish the importance of natural family relationships. Rather, it reveals that the mission of God establishes a deeper form of spiritual kinship that transcends biological ties.

2. The Centrality of the Will of God

“whoever does the will of God”

The defining characteristic of membership in the spiritual family of Christ is obedience to the will of God. In biblical theology, the will of God refers to the divine purpose and moral direction revealed through God’s commands and redemptive plan.
Obedience to this will demonstrates genuine allegiance to God and alignment with His kingdom. Thus Christ emphasizes that belonging to the family of God is not determined by lineage or heritage but by faithful submission to the purposes of God.

3. The Formation of a New Community

By identifying His followers as brother, sister, and mother, Christ introduces the concept of a new covenant community. This community is characterized by shared devotion to God rather than by traditional social structures. The early Christian community embraced this understanding by referring to fellow believers as brothers and sisters in Christ. Such language reflects the deep relational bond created through shared faith and obedience. Thus the kingdom of God establishes a family defined by spiritual commitment rather than by bloodline.

4. The Equality Within the Spiritual Family

Christ’s statement includes male and female members of the community, identifying them equally as participants in the family of God. This inclusion underscores the universal nature of the kingdom. In the spiritual family established by Christ, distinctions of ethnicity, social status, and gender do not determine one’s standing before God. All who obey the will of God share equally in the privileges and responsibilities of belonging to His family.

5. The Christological Dimension

The spiritual family described in this passage is formed through relationship with Jesus Christ. Christ serves as the center and unifying figure of this community. Through His teaching, ministry, death, and resurrection, Christ gathers individuals into a new covenant family united by faith and obedience. Thus the community of believers becomes a living testimony to the transformative power of the kingdom of God.

Greek Notes

Several Greek expressions illuminate the theological meaning of this verse.

“ποιήσῃ” (poiēsē)
Translated “does,” this verb refers to active obedience and faithful practice rather than mere intellectual agreement.

“θέλημα τοῦ Θεοῦ” (thelēma tou Theou)
Meaning “the will of God,” this phrase refers to the divine purpose and moral instruction revealed through God’s commands and redemptive plan.

“ἀδελφός … ἀδελφή … μήτηρ” (adelphos … adelphē … mētēr)
Translated “brother, sister, and mother,” these relational terms emphasize the familial nature of the community formed around Christ.

Together these expressions emphasize that spiritual kinship is defined by active obedience to the will of God within the community gathered around Christ.

Ministry Implications

For students and ministers trained within Tenio Theological University and the mission of Narrow Gate Christian Ministry, this passage provides several important insights.

1. The Church Is a Spiritual Family
Christian communities should cultivate relationships that reflect the deep spiritual kinship described by Christ.

2. Obedience Defines Authentic Discipleship
Belonging to the family of God is demonstrated through faithful obedience to His will.

3. Ministry Must Welcome All Who Seek to Follow God
The spiritual family of God transcends social and cultural barriers, welcoming all who commit themselves to His will.

4. Community Strengthens Faithful Living
The fellowship of believers provides encouragement, accountability, and support in pursuing the will of God.

Concluding Reflection

In the teaching recorded in the Gospel of Mark, Jesus Christ reveals that the deepest bonds of human relationship are formed not merely through biological connection but through shared obedience to God. Those who align their lives with the will of God become members of a new and enduring family centered upon Christ. Within this spiritual family, believers find fellowship, encouragement, and shared participation in the mission of God. Thus the community of faith stands as a living testimony that the kingdom of God creates relationships that transcend earthly boundaries and unite believers in the common pursuit of the will of God.
