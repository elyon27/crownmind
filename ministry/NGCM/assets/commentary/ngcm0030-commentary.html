The Principle of Spiritual Reception: The Increase of Understanding in the Kingdom

A Theological Commentary on Matthew 13:12

TTU Exegetical Commentary

“For whoever has, to him more will be given, and he will have abundance; but whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken away from him.”
— Gospel of Matthew 13:12

Key Doctrine

The Principle of Spiritual Responsiveness in Receiving Divine Revelation

In this statement, Jesus Christ articulates a fundamental principle governing the reception of divine truth. Within the context of His teaching on parables, Christ reveals that spiritual understanding grows in those who respond faithfully to the truth they receive, while those who reject or neglect that truth gradually lose their capacity for understanding. The doctrine emphasized in this passage is that spiritual illumination increases through faithful reception and obedience to God’s revelation, whereas resistance to truth results in progressive spiritual blindness. Thus the kingdom of God operates according to a dynamic principle in which the heart’s response to truth determines the degree of further revelation granted.

Exposition
1. The Context of the Parables

The statement recorded in Gospel of Matthew 13:12 occurs within Christ’s explanation of why He teaches in parables. After presenting the Parable of the Sower, the disciples ask why Christ communicates His teaching through symbolic narratives rather than direct instruction. Christ explains that the parables function both to reveal and to conceal truth. Those who approach the message with receptive hearts gain deeper understanding, while those whose hearts are hardened remain unable to perceive the significance of the message. Thus the parables serve as a means of testing the spiritual receptivity of the listener.

2. The Meaning of “Whoever Has”

“For whoever has”

The phrase refers not to material possessions but to spiritual receptivity and understanding. Those who “have” are individuals who receive the message of the kingdom with humility and openness. Possessing this initial understanding enables them to grow further in knowledge and spiritual maturity. Thus “having” refers to the possession of a receptive heart capable of responding to divine revelation.

3. The Principle of Increase

“to him more will be given”

Christ describes a dynamic process in which spiritual understanding expands as individuals faithfully respond to the truth they receive. Those who embrace the message of the kingdom and apply it to their lives experience deeper insight and greater participation in the realities of God’s reign. This principle reflects the nature of spiritual growth: obedience to revealed truth opens the door to greater revelation. Thus divine knowledge is not merely accumulated intellectually but deepened through faithful response and lived experience.

4. The Reality of Spiritual Loss

“whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken away”

This statement introduces the sobering reality that spiritual neglect leads to diminishing perception. Individuals who resist or ignore divine truth gradually lose the clarity they once possessed. Over time, indifference toward spiritual realities produces a hardened heart that becomes increasingly resistant to God’s voice. Thus the verse describes the progressive nature of spiritual decline when truth is rejected.

5. The Moral Responsibility of Hearing

The principle articulated by Christ highlights the responsibility that accompanies exposure to divine revelation. Hearing the truth of God carries with it an obligation to respond with humility and obedience. The response of the listener determines whether the truth becomes a source of illumination or a point of judgment. Thus the parables serve both as an invitation to deeper understanding and as a warning against spiritual complacency.

Greek Notes

Several Greek expressions illuminate the meaning of this passage.

“ὃς γὰρ ἔχει” (hos gar echei)
Translated “whoever has,” this phrase refers to possessing spiritual understanding or receptivity.

“δοθήσεται” (dothēsetai)
Meaning “will be given,” this future passive verb indicates that the increase in understanding originates from God.

“περισσευθήσεται” (perisseuthēsetai)
Translated “will have abundance,” this term emphasizes the overflowing nature of spiritual growth granted by God.

“ἀρθήσεται” (arthēsetai)
Meaning “will be taken away,” this verb describes the loss of understanding experienced by those who reject or neglect truth.

Together these expressions highlight the dynamic nature of spiritual perception and the consequences of one’s response to divine revelation.

Ministry Implications

For students and ministers trained within Tenio Theological University and the mission of Narrow Gate Christian Ministry, this passage provides several critical insights.

1. Spiritual Growth Requires Active Response to Truth
Understanding the Word of God must lead to obedience and transformation rather than remaining merely intellectual.

2. Teaching Must Cultivate Receptive Hearts
Effective ministry involves preparing listeners to receive and respond to the message of the kingdom.

3. Neglect of Truth Leads to Spiritual Decline
Failure to respond to divine revelation can gradually harden the heart and diminish spiritual perception.

4. Discipleship Encourages Continual Growth
Believers are called to deepen their understanding of God’s Word through ongoing learning, reflection, and faithful practice.

Concluding Reflection

In the teaching recorded in the Gospel of Matthew, Jesus Christ reveals a profound spiritual principle governing the reception of divine truth. Those who respond to God’s revelation with humility and obedience experience an ever-increasing understanding of the kingdom. Yet those who resist or neglect the truth gradually lose the clarity they once possessed. Thus the words of Christ remind believers that spiritual understanding is not merely a matter of intellectual knowledge but of the heart’s posture toward God.

Where there is openness and obedience, truth multiplies.
Where there is resistance, light fades into darkness.
